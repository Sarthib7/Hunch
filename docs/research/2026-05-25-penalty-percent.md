# Research — Soft pari-mutuel penalty % for Hunch price markets

- **For:** [ADR-0014](../adr/0014-price-prediction-surface.md)
- **Recommendation:** **30%** (medium confidence)

> Auto-generated by a research subagent on 2026-05-25 during the W2 grilling
> session. Preserved verbatim for the historical trail; the conclusion is
> incorporated into ADR-0014. If we ever revisit the penalty %, start here.

## Recommendation: 30% — confidence medium

### Why

1. **Loss aversion math fits.** Kahneman-Tversky's lambda ≈ 2.25 means a 30%
   real loss feels like ~67.5% pain — uncomfortable but not crushing. At 50%
   it crosses the psychological 100% line ("feels like total loss") and the
   game starts to read as gambling. Recent meta-analysis suggests lambda is
   closer to 1.5–2.0, which makes 30% even safer.

2. **Headline payout sells the game.** In the balanced 10v10 case at p=30%,
   winners earn **+15%** in one minute — the screenshot-worthy number. At
   p=20% it's +10% (boring); at p=50% it's +25% (good but loss bleeds).
   30% sits in the sweet spot of generous-on-win, survivable-on-loss.

3. **A balanced market is zero-EV regardless of p.** Every 1 CRC of loser
   forfeit flows back to a winner. With a mildly biased crowd (60/40 avg
   lean) and 50% accuracy, a user actually has slight **positive** EV
   (~+0.6 CRC over 50 markets at p=30%) because half the time they're on
   the contrarian/minority side capturing the bigger bonus. Burn is not
   the binding constraint; **variance** is.

4. **Variance is tolerable at 30%.** Stdev of net result over 50 balanced
   markets at 50% accuracy = ±2.12 CRC at p=30% (vs ±7.07 at p=100%). A
   user is extremely unlikely to bankrupt out — bad streaks sting but
   recover.

5. **Precedents tilt toward "low friction."** Polymarket / Kalshi take
   essentially 100% loss but charge ~2% vig. SX Bet aims for <0.5% vig.
   Horse-racing pari-mutuel takes 15–26% (the takeout). No clean precedent
   found for a soft *redistributive* pari-mutuel — closest analogue is
   binary-options cashback at 15–25%. 30% places Hunch in the horse-track
   comfort band but with **upside redistribution** rather than house take.

(Imbalanced-market check: at 95 UP / 5 DOWN, UP-winner payout is 1.016 CRC at

consensus markets.)

## Alternative — variable penalty scaling with crowd imbalance

Anchor p=30% at 50/50 splits; ramp up to p=50% as the market grows
imbalanced (`p_effective = 0.30 + 0.40 × |majority_frac − 0.5|`). This:
- Keeps the headline 1.15× payout in balanced markets
- Punishes lemming behaviour (joining the obvious 95% side gets you ~1%
  upside but a 50% loss if wrong)
- Rewards contrarian conviction in genuinely asymmetric markets
- Mirrors LMSR pricing intuition without the math overhead

Cost: harder to explain in onboarding ("why was it 22% sometimes and 30%
other times?"). **Defer to v1.1** once static-30% baseline data exists.

## Gotchas (carried into ADR-0014)

- **One-sided markets pay almost nothing regardless of p.** At 95/5
  splits, winner upside is ~1.5–5% across all reasonable p. The penalty
  knob doesn't fix this — it's structural to pari-mutuel. **Mitigation:**
  show implied probability up front so users self-route toward contested
  markets.

- **Pyth resolution variance.** Pyth Lazer sub-400ms latency at 99.9%
  uptime, but on 1-minute markets a single bad tick at the boundary
  determines the outcome. **Pin a specific `publish_time` window** (e.g.,
  the first valid update at or after `minute_boundary + 1s`) and ship a
  public "how resolution works" doc. Disputes at 30% penalty hurt less
  than at 100% — a *side benefit* of going soft.

- **Latency-arbitrage bots.** A Polymarket user reportedly turned $300
  into $400k on 15-min BTC/ETH markets via cross-exchange latency arb
  (Feb 2026). 1-min markets are even more vulnerable. **Consider closing
  voting 10–15s before the minute boundary** to compress the arb window.

- **Indexer lag at high volume.** At 2,880 markets/day you'll quickly
  outpace `PAYOUT_BATCH_LIMIT = 20` in `lib/round/payout.ts`. **Bump to
  200+** or move to a queue worker before launch.

- **Sybil + cooldown interaction.** The 1-h voter cooldown doesn't bind
  if attackers spin up multiple trust-verified avatars across days. Soft
  penalty *reduces* Sybil ROI (each fake avatar only earns 15% in a
  balanced market, not 100%) but also reduces the cost of vote-stuffing
  one side to skew the market. Worth monitoring once W2 ships.

- **Don't conflate "soft" with "safe".** A 30% penalty is still real
  money on a trust-graph currency some users earned through real human
  relationships. **Frame penalties in the UI as "shared with winners"**
  not "burned" — keeps the redistributive ethos visible.
